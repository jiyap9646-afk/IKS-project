Foundations of Ayurveda

Ayurveda is based on the concept of five elements (earth, water, fire, air, space) and their combination into three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha. Health was believed to depend on keeping these doshas in balance through diet, lifestyle, herbs, and therapies. Treatments and Practices

Ayurveda included herbal medicines, oils, yoga, and meditation for physical and mental well-being. Therapies like Panchakarma (detoxification) were used for cleansing the body. Other treatments included leech therapy, detox diets, and specialized massages. Surgery, pioneered by Sushruta, included techniques like cataract removal and plastic surgery, centuries before such methods appeared in Europe.

Global Influence

Ayurveda spread to Tibet, China, Sri Lanka, and later the Middle East. Through translations into Persian and Arabic, it influenced Greek and Roman medicine. Arab scholars like Avicenna drew upon Ayurvedic principles, which later reached Europe. In modern times, Ayurveda has influenced practices such as homeopathy, naturopathy, and integrative medicine.

Timeline of Ayurveda

  • 1500 BCE: Early references to healing practices in the Vedas.
  • 600 BCE: Sushruta Samhita introduces advanced surgery.
  • 100 CE: Charaka Samhita compiles knowledge on internal medicine.
  • 500 CE: Ashtanga Hridaya written by Vagbhata.
  • 9th Century CE: Ayurveda spreads through translations into Arabic and Persian.
